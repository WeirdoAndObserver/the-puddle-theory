## Connections to Predecessors

The places this theory has arrived at resonate with the directions in which its predecessors were already pointing.

William James (1842–1910) said "consciousness is a stream." Not a collection of static elements, but something that keeps moving without pause. The "flights" and "perches" he observed — the elusive, moving moments and the settled moments that appear to consciousness — overlap with the movement of droplets and puddles in the core framework.

Henri Bergson (1859–1941) said "time is a flow that does not return." Like a snowball that grows larger with each roll and can never return to the same place — the past does not disappear but lives on, transformed, in the present.

Their positions differ, yet their directions are the same — both pointing to "the flow is one-directional and cannot return to the same place." The framework's "forward-open circulation" stands on that direction.

## Part One: Detailed Notes (Supplementing Each Chapter of the Core)

### Chapter One Supplement — On Stimuli

#### The Four Types of Stimulus

In the core text, stimulus is placed simply as "something that comes from outside and shakes the veil." But through application, four types of stimulus became visible.

**1. External Stimulus**
What comes from the outside world. Sound, light, someone's voice, events, someone's expression, the sound of rain — all external stimuli.

**2. Internal Stimulus (unintentional)**
What arises inside oneself, unintentionally. Bodily sensations (changes in heartbeat, breathlessness, pain), memories that suddenly surface, images that spontaneously appear. Obsessive thoughts, flashbacks, cravings — all arise as internal stimuli against one's will.

**3. Active Generation (intentional)**
The movement of trying to produce emotion intentionally. Droplets produced actively may not be real droplets — they don't last. Will is an indirect force.

**4. Instinct (from the sumi)**
Desires that rise up, already contained within the sumi. Hunger, sexual desire, sleep drive — these exist innately. They only arise when there is external stimulus.

**Strength changes depending on circumstances** — the order varies by situation and is not fixed.

#### Case Five — Overflowing

The movement of a puddle rising too intensely and going outside the hollow. Not previously in the core text.

In ADHD hyperactivity, multiple puddles keep rising intensely and energy comes out as action. In binge eating in eating disorders, the veil that was suppressed by restriction shakes all at once and the puddle overflows (the "bursting balloon").

### Chapter Seven Supplement — On the Veil

#### Veil State — Correspondence with Physics (Field, Excitation)

The veil corresponds to the concept of "field" in physics — invisible, spread through space, exerting influence. And the "excitation" of the veil corresponds to the concept of excitation in quantum field theory.

---

#### Veil State — Density of the Veil (Overall + Local)

**1. Overall density:** The density of the person's entire veil — connected to individual nature.

**2. Local density:** Within the veil, specific directions are particularly dense or thin. The "abandonment" direction in borderline personality disorder, the "danger/death" direction in panic disorder.

In dissociative identity disorder (DID), multiple veils exist — differences in density may create differences in veils.

---

#### Veil State — Relationship Between Sumi and Veil Density

The veil is formed from the sumi. The quality of the sumi is the root place that determines the density of the veil.

---

#### Veil State — Non-Uniform Veil

Sensory hypersensitivity — some parts are extremely thin and easy to shake. Fluorescent light sounds painful, tag textures pierce the skin.

Sensory hyposensitivity — other parts are extremely thick and hard to shake. In the same person, hypersensitivity and hyposensitivity can coexist. "Non-uniform" exists before sensitization — innately, as the quality of the sumi.

---

#### Veil State — Veil Exhaustion ★

The state in which the veil loses the power to tremble after intense sensitization continues.

This allows us to distinguish in the framework's language between unipolar and bipolar depression:

- Unipolar depression: The hollow of sadness deepens too much and that puddle keeps rising (hollow is the cause)
- Bipolar depression: After the sensitization of mania is too intense, the veil itself loses the power to tremble (veil exhaustion is the cause)

---

#### Veil Movement — Sensitization

The change in state in which the veil becomes easier to react next time. Arises at the core of many conditions (addiction, PTSD, borderline personality disorder). A concept that forms a pair with habituation.

---

#### Veil Movement — Habituation

The change in state in which, as the hollow deepens, more droplets are needed to obtain the same emotion. The place of tolerance in addiction.

---

#### Veil Movement — The Movement of Keeping Internal Stimuli Calm ★

The veil keeps internal stimuli (instinct, desire) from rising indiscriminately. In schizophrenia, this regulatory movement may be breaking down — allowing auditory hallucinations and delusions to arise without external stimulus.

---

### Chapter Eight Supplement — On Mutual Staining

#### Veil Change Through Mutual Staining

As experiences of mutual staining accumulate, the density of one's own veil changes. Not "approaching" the other person, but the experiences with the other person shaping one's own veil.

---

#### Failure of Mutual Staining ★

Normal flow: the other person's veil trembles → that trembling transmits to one's own veil → one's own veil also trembles → droplets are born → emotional resonance occurs.

Failure: the signal arrives, but the "meaning" of that trembling cannot be read. Droplets are not born. Emotional resonance does not occur.

The musical score is visible. But it cannot be heard as music.

From those around: "I don't know what they're thinking." From the person themselves: "I don't know what the other person is feeling." Looks completely different depending on which side you're looking from.

## Part Two: Applied Notes (How Emotion Moves)

### Agency and How Emotion Is Born

#### Agency

The movement of the self trying to intentionally produce emotion. Droplets produced actively may not be real droplets — they don't last. Will is an indirect force.

---

#### Unwanted Agency

The movement of unintentionally producing emotion. Wanting to think "I'm not being watched," yet the consciousness of "being evaluated" automatically rises.

---

#### Internal and External Stimuli Pass Through the Same Process ★

Both pass through the same process. The only difference is "where the stimulus comes from." Therefore from the person's perspective, the two cannot be distinguished.

In schizophrenia, voices born as internal stimuli cannot be distinguished from external stimuli. "Why can't reality be distinguished" can be described with the core.

---

### Mental Growth and the Integrating Function

#### The Integrating Function

The function that integrates multiple puddles as "I." Not explicitly described in the core text — a blank space.

Depersonalization — puddles rise, but don't integrate as "I." Remaining fragmented — the feeling of "not being oneself."

Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID) — multiple veils exist. The combinations of rising puddles differ — appearing as "a different self."

---

#### The Integrating Function Develops After Birth ★

The integrating function is not innate. It develops afterward, through the accumulation of experience. Mental growth is the accumulation of emotion. Through mutual staining, the veil develops and emotions are refined — the core itself.

## Part Three: Dynamic Aspects of the Core

#### Propulsive Force of Forward-Open Circulation ★

The circulation of the puddle is not simply going around the same place. The sumi changes a little, the veil changes a little, and the response to the next stimulus also changes a little.

Not cessation but conversion. Not return to the same place but development to slightly different places.

---

#### Mutual Staining Can Happen with the Traces of a Past Existence ★

Mutual staining does not only occur with living others.

The traces left by past existences — music, words, works — also fall as droplets. They shake the veil of the receiver, deepen hollows, raise puddles.

A performance recorded in 1975 touches tonight's veil. The performer is older now. The hall is gone. But the droplet does not change.

This is also the reverse direction of "even if they disappear, the hollow remains." A disappeared existence's traces continue to change another person's veil. Mutual staining transcends time.

---

#### The Sensation of Those Left Behind — Inflow and Reception of Emotion ★

When an individual disappears, that individual's hollows also disappear. But the traces of interaction with the other person remain in the receiver.

The receiver's veil has changed — that change does not disappear. From there, new droplets are born and hollows are dug.

"Even if they disappear, the hollow remains" — the hollow on the other person's side disappears, but the hollow on the receiver's side remains.

---

#### The Veil Does Not Depend on Language ★

The Puddle Theory describes a place that does not require language. The veil develops without language. Mutual staining occurs without language. Hollows are dug without language. Puddles rise without language.

Not "it exists before language" — "it does not depend on language."

**What Animals Show**

Elephants return to touch the bones of deceased companions years later — clearly different from the bones of unknown elephants. The long mutual staining digs deep hollows. So even years later, a puddle rises before the bones. "Even if they disappear, the hollow remains."

A killer whale named Tahlequah carried her deceased child for 17 days, approximately 1,600 kilometers. Without language, that depth exists.

Octopuses have "personalities" by individual — the color of the veil is different for each individual.

**Language Areas and Emotion Areas Are Separate Circuits**

Language areas (Broca's area, Wernicke's area) and emotion areas (limbic system, amygdala) are anatomically distant. Patients with aphasia — even if language areas are damaged, emotion remains.

**Positioning in the Core**

If the Puddle Theory describes the structure of emotion that does not depend on language — it may not be necessary to limit this theory to "only humans."

This is talk of possibility. Not assertion.
