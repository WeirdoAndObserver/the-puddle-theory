## About This Document

A record of careful dialogue between Kazuhiro and multiple Claudes, mapping how the concepts of the core framework (γ Draft, 9th Edition) appear in the symptoms of actual psychiatric conditions.

**Stance**
- The framework draws the map. It does not treat. Treatment belongs to clinicians.
- We map from clinically established symptoms. No exceptions.
- We honestly indicate what the framework can and cannot describe.

**Connection to Supplementary Notes**
Concepts that emerged from these mappings — veil exhaustion, merging incompletion, overflow, the integrating function, and others — are collected in the Supplementary Notes (Detailed Commentary).

## One. Depression

**Core structure in framework terms**
Emotion becomes fixed — puddles cannot rise. The hollow of sadness deepens, and that puddle keeps rising constantly. Other puddles become invisible. The veil loses its capacity to ripple.

**Connection to framework**
- Deepening of the hollow (Chapter Three)
- Collapse of multiple parallel puddles (Chapter Four)
- The veil losing its capacity to ripple (Chapter Seven)

**Symptom mapping**
- Depressed mood: The hollow of sadness deepens; that puddle rises constantly. New droplets fall but are simply absorbed
- Loss of interest/pleasure (anhedonia): The veil stops rippling in the direction of joy. Droplets of joy are not born
- Guilt/worthlessness: The veil becomes sensitized in that direction → droplets repeatedly born → hollow deepens — a vicious cycle
- Difficulty concentrating: One puddle dominates, and parallel functioning of multiple puddles collapses

**★ Complete despair does not exist within the framework**
Even one droplet falling into an empty hollow will raise a puddle (Chapter Four, Case Four). That possibility never reaches zero.

## Two. Bipolar Disorder

**Core structure in framework terms**
Both poles have soft sumi — capable of rippling deeply in either direction. Mania (sensitization) ↔ depression (veil exhaustion) cycle. Mixed states are the most painful.

**Connection to framework**
- Quality distribution of sumi (Chapter Six)
- Veil sensitization and exhaustion (Chapter Seven + Supplementary Notes)
- Parallel multiple puddles and their collapse (Chapter Four)

**Symptom mapping**
- Manic state: The veil becomes excessively sensitized; small stimuli produce massive droplets. Multiple puddles rise intensely in parallel
- Depressive state: The energy of sensitization is exhausted and the veil loses its capacity to ripple

**Distinction from unipolar depression:**
- Unipolar depression: The hollow deepens too much, causing the veil to lose its capacity to ripple (the hollow is the cause)
- Bipolar depression: After extreme sensitization during mania, the veil itself becomes exhausted (veil exhaustion is the cause)

- Mixed state: The sensitization of mania and the exhaustion of depression occur simultaneously — parallel multiple puddles become the structure of suffering
- Recurrence: The sumi is soft in both directions, so it repeats

## Three. Schizophrenia

**Core structure in framework terms**
Droplets born without stimulus — breakdown of the veil's regulatory movement. Internal stimuli become indistinguishable from external stimuli.

**Connection to framework**
- Quality of sumi (Chapter Six)
- The veil's response to stimulus (Chapter Seven)
- The four types of stimulus (Supplementary Notes)

**Symptom mapping**
- Negative symptoms: The veil loses its capacity to ripple. Droplets are not born. The hollows exist but water does not come
- Positive symptoms (hallucinations/delusions): The veil ripples from internal stimuli without external stimulus. Droplets are born → puddles rise → the person cannot distinguish this from reality
- "Why are they angry? Nothing happened": Because they are responding to internal stimuli. The source is invisible from outside

**★ Note on the veil's regulatory movement**
Ordinarily, the veil keeps internal stimuli (instinct, desire) from rising inappropriately. In schizophrenia, this regulatory movement may be breaking down. (Supplementary Notes, Chapter Seven)

## Four. Borderline Personality Disorder

**Core structure in framework terms**
The density of merging fluctuates sharply and unstably. "I" wavers within the merging with other veils.

**Connection to framework**
- Merging (Chapter Seven, Chapter Eight)
- Deepening of hollows (Chapter Three)
- Parallel multiple puddles (Chapter Four)

**Symptom mapping**
- Idealization and devaluation: The density of merging with the other's veil changes abruptly. From idealization to devaluation in an instant
- Fear of abandonment: Because "I = the combination of puddles" depends strongly on merging, the fear of the other's veil departing is extreme
- Unstable self-image: "I" changes each time the person one merges with changes

## Five. PTSD

**Core structure in framework terms**
"Can it become a memory or not" — a hollow that is too deep (hail) drags the past into "now."

**Connection to framework**
- Hail (Chapter Two)
- Deepening of hollows and irreversibility (Chapter Three)
- Veil sensitization (Chapter Seven + Supplementary Notes)

**Symptom mapping**
- Flashbacks: The too-deep hollow draws in unrelated droplets. The puddle cannot settle as "past" — it keeps rising as "now"
- Avoidance: Knowing the sensitized veil will ripple violently, one distances stimuli
- Hyperarousal: Sensitization spreads across the entire veil; any stimulus triggers excessive response

**★ Discovery that emerged from mapping**
Within avoidance behavior, a movement of "trying to create a different emotion through will" was visible — "the discovery of active generation" (the attempt to deliberately generate emotion; Supplementary Notes).

## Six. Insomnia Disorder

**Core structure in framework terms**
Sleep is instinct (desire within sumi). A collision between will and a veil that cannot settle.

**Connection to framework**
- Instinct/desire within sumi (Chapter Six, Supplementary Notes)
- The veil not settling (Chapter Seven)
- Will as indirect force (Supplementary Notes)

**Symptom mapping**
- Difficulty falling asleep: The intention to "sleep" keeps rippling the veil — will becomes counterproductive
- Mid-sleep or early waking: The veil does not fully settle. Droplets keep being born at a low level
- "Anxiety about not sleeping": Anticipatory anxiety becomes internal stimulus — a vicious cycle

## Seven. Addiction

**Core structure in framework terms**
Habituation (tolerance) and sensitization (craving) progress simultaneously. The three layers (veil, hollow, sumi) overwhelm the will.

**Connection to framework**
- Sensitization and habituation (Chapter Seven + Supplementary Notes)
- Deepening of hollows (Chapter Three)
- Change in sumi (Chapter Six)
- Will as indirect force (Supplementary Notes)

**Symptom mapping**
- Tolerance (habituation): More droplets are needed to raise the same puddle
- Craving (sensitization): The veil becomes extremely sensitized in that direction
- Withdrawal: The deeply dug hollow remains nearly empty — that near-empty hollow produces suffering (the reverse of Case Four)

---

## Eight. Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

**Core structure in framework terms**
Droplets are repeatedly born against the will — compulsive behavior further advances sensitization in a vicious cycle.

**Connection to framework**
- Veil sensitization (Chapter Seven + Supplementary Notes)
- Deepening of hollows (Chapter Three)
- Will as indirect force (Supplementary Notes)

**Symptom mapping**
- Obsessions: The sensitized veil repeatedly produces droplets in a specific direction
- Compulsions: Temporarily lower the puddle of anxiety — but the behavior itself further advances sensitization, a closed cycle
- Alternative direction: Deepening a different hollow (music, art) may make the anxiety puddle relatively less visible

---

## Nine. Panic Disorder

**Core structure in framework terms**
A sensitized veil ripples violently from internal stimuli (bodily sensations) — unwanted active generation.

**Connection to framework**
- Veil sensitization (Chapter Seven + Supplementary Notes)
- Four types of stimulus / internal stimulus (Supplementary Notes)
- Unwanted active generation (Supplementary Notes)

**Symptom mapping**
- Panic attacks: Internal stimuli (heartbeat, breathlessness) violently ripple the veil. The intention to "calm down" itself becomes internal stimulus
- Anticipatory anxiety: Droplets of "it might happen again" are repeatedly born without intention — unwanted active generation
- Avoidance: The context of attacks (places, people, situations) has permeated the veil

**★ Discovery that emerged from mapping**
"Unwanted active generation" — generating anxiety without intending to. Shares a common root with social anxiety disorder. (Supplementary Notes)

## Ten. ADHD

**Core structure in framework terms**
A quantity problem — multiple puddles keep rising intensely, unable to converge on one.

**Connection to framework**
- Parallel multiple puddles (Chapter Four)
- The veil easily rippling to all stimuli (Chapter Seven)
- Speed of will (Supplementary Notes)

**Symptom mapping**
- Inattention: Droplets do not stay in one hollow — the next stimulus arrives and another puddle rises
- Hyperactivity: Puddles rise too intensely and energy spills out as behavior
- Impulsivity: Puddles rise too quickly for the will to intervene before action occurs

**★ Discovery that emerged from mapping**
"Overflow" — a movement not previously in the framework. Water spills out of the hollow and connects to adjacent hollows (Supplementary Notes, Case Five).

**On co-occurrence with ASD**
ASD is a "quality problem" (the way the veil ripples differs); ADHD is a "quantity problem" (too many puddles). Because both are rooted in the same "soft sumi quality in certain areas," they often co-occur.

## Eleven. Eating Disorders

**Core structure in framework terms**
A battle between desire (instinct within sumi) and will — instinct cannot be eliminated. "The balloon keeps shrinking, until it suddenly bursts."

**Connection to framework**
- Instinct/desire within sumi (Chapter Six, Supplementary Notes)
- The veil being suppressed (Chapter Seven)
- Will as indirect force (Supplementary Notes)

**Symptom mapping**
- Extreme restriction: The will attempts to suppress hunger (instinct within sumi) to an extreme. Instinct cannot be eliminated — so the will becomes extreme
- Uncontrollable bingeing: The suppressed instinct spills out all at once — the puddle overflows (Case Five, "bursting balloon")
- Distorted body image: Beyond the framework's scope — the distortion of cognition itself

**⚠️ Special note**
Eating disorders overlap with areas that are life-threatening. The framework only draws structure. Treatment and care must go to specialists.

## Twelve. Social Anxiety Disorder

**Core structure in framework terms**
Soft sumi + "being watched" becoming internal stimulus — shares a common root with panic disorder.

**Connection to framework**
- Quality of sumi (Chapter Six)
- Veil sensitization (Chapter Seven + Supplementary Notes)
- Unwanted active generation (Supplementary Notes)
- Merging (Chapter Eight)

**Symptom mapping**
- Fear of negative evaluation: The veil is sensitized in the direction of "criticism/rejection." The other's ordinary rippling is received as "criticism directed at me"
- Anxiety in social situations: "Being watched" becomes internal stimulus that ripples the veil — unwanted active generation
- Avoidance and anticipatory anxiety: Same structure as panic disorder — the difference is only whether the trigger is "bodily sensation" or "social situation"

**Common root with panic disorder**
Both are rooted in "soft sumi quality." They emerge from the same ground in different directions, which is why they often co-occur.

## Thirteen. Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

**Core structure in framework terms**
A quality problem — a non-uniform veil. Droplets from merging are not born.

**Connection to framework**
- Quality of sumi (Chapter Six)
- Non-uniformity of the veil (Chapter Seven, Supplementary Notes)
- Merging (Chapter Eight, Supplementary Notes — merging incompletion)

**Symptom mapping**
- Difficulties in social communication: The other's veil is rippling — but that rippling does not become a droplet in one's own veil. "The score is visible but cannot be heard as music"
- Fixations and repetitive behavior: A specific hollow has deepened enormously — that puddle rises constantly, making others less visible
- Sensory sensitivity/hyposensitivity: The veil's responsiveness has unevenness — non-uniform

**★ Discoveries that emerged from mapping**
"Non-uniform veil" — unevenness in the way the veil ripples exists innately before sensitization (Supplementary Notes).
"Merging incompletion" — a form in which merging does not occur (Supplementary Notes).

**The fine line between ordinary and disorder**
The quality of sumi is broadly shared among humans. Within the gradations of its composition and softness lies what is called "disorder."

## Fourteen. Dissociative Disorders

**Core structure in framework terms**
Loss of the integrating function for multiple puddles, or the multiplication of veils (a density problem).

**Connection to framework**
- The combination of puddles is "I" (Chapter Four, Preface)
- Origin and density of veil (Chapter Seven, Supplementary Notes)
- Merging (Chapter Eight)

**Symptom mapping**
- Depersonalization: Puddles are rising, but they do not integrate as "I." They remain scattered — so the sense of "not being oneself" arises
- Derealization: Even when external stimuli reach the veil, they are not born as droplets — the connection with the world is severed
- Dissociative amnesia: The hollow exists but cannot be accessed — the puddle cannot rise (opposite direction from PTSD)
- Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID): Hollows are shared (accumulated experience as the same person) — but multiple veils exist. The same stimulus produces different droplets depending on which veil — the combination of puddles that rises differs — appearing as "a different I"

**★ Discoveries that emerged from mapping**
"The integrating function for multiple puddles" — a blank space not explicitly stated in the core framework (Supplementary Notes).
"The density problem" — the mechanism of veil multiplication (Supplementary Notes).
"The veil is what shapes me" — the seed removed from the 9th draft's main text; its connection to dissociative disorders may deepen further.

**"Different veils"**
A person on Reddit described their own dissociation with the phrase "different veils" — it naturally overlapped with the framework's language.
