## Connections to Predecessors

The places this theory has arrived at resonate with the directions in which its predecessors were already pointing.

William James (1842–1910) said "consciousness is a stream." Not a collection of static elements, but something that keeps moving without pause. The "flights" and "perches" he observed — the elusive, moving moments and the settled moments that appear to consciousness — overlap with the movement of droplets and puddles in the core framework.

Henri Bergson (1859–1941) said "time is a flow that does not return." Like a snowball that grows larger with each roll and can never return to the same place — the past does not disappear but lives on, transformed, in the present.

Their positions differ, yet their directions are the same — both pointing to "the flow is one-directional and cannot return to the same place." The framework's "forward-open circulation" stands on that direction.

## Part One: Detailed Notes (Supplementing Each Chapter of the Core)

### Chapter One Supplement — On Stimuli

#### The Four Types of Stimulus

In the core text, stimulus is placed simply as "something that comes from outside and shakes the veil." But through application, four types of stimulus became visible.

**1. External Stimulus**
What comes from the outside world. Sound, light, someone's voice, events, someone's expression, the sound of rain — all external stimuli. The most basic stimulus described in the core as the place where the veil is shaken.

**2. Internal Stimulus (unintentional)**
What arises inside oneself, unintentionally. Bodily sensations (changes in heartbeat, breathlessness, pain), memories that suddenly surface, images that spontaneously appear in one's mind — not stimuli from outside, but functioning as places where the veil is shaken. Obsessive thoughts, flashbacks, cravings — all arise as internal stimuli. The place that is born against one's will and cannot be stopped.

**3. Active Generation (intentional)**
The movement of trying to produce emotion intentionally. Trying to create enjoyment by thinking "let's have fun." Trying to produce calm by thinking "let me settle down." Droplets produced actively may not be real droplets — they don't last, they disappear quickly. Will is an indirect force.

**4. Instinct (from the ink)**
Desires that rise up, already contained within the ink. Hunger, sexual desire, sleep drive — these exist innately within the ink. They normally remain on standby. They only arise when there is external stimulus (seeing food, when night comes).

**Strength changes depending on circumstances**

The initial placement of "order of strength: instinct > internal stimulus > external stimulus > active generation" is not definitive. In practice, it changes depending on the situation.

- Pattern 1: Instinct is strongest (unfulfilled hunger, sleep drive overwhelms everything)
- Pattern 2: External stimulus is strongest (hail, explosion, severe pain)
- Pattern 3: Will suppresses instinct (anorexia — but if it continues, instinct overflows)
- Pattern 4: Internal stimulus is indistinguishable from external stimulus (auditory hallucinations in schizophrenia)
- Pattern 5: Agency functions (as an example, the movement of deepening another hollow, long-term development in another direction, a place that exists as possibility)

**Connection to Core:** Chapter 1 "Stimulus," Chapter 6 "Ink," Chapter 7 "Veil." The stimulus in the main text was "from outside," but through application it expanded to include the inside, instinct, and agency.

**Origin:** Distinction between external and internal stimulus: from OCD, addiction, PTSD. Active generation: from PTSD ("Discovery of Agency," June 16, 2026). Instinct (from ink): from insomnia, addiction, eating disorders.

### Chapter Four Supplement — On Puddles

#### Case Five — Overflowing (Extension of Chapter Four Cases)

The movement of a puddle rising too intensely and going outside the hollow. The core's Chapter Four describes Cases One through Four, but this is the fifth place that arises as an extension of those.

A movement not previously in the core text — droplets "falling" and puddles "rising" were there. But "overflowing" was not.

In the case of ADHD hyperactivity, multiple puddles keep rising intensely and energy comes out as action. In the case of binge eating in eating disorders, the veil that was suppressed by restriction shakes all at once and the puddle overflows (the metaphor of a balloon bursting).

**Connection to Core:** Close to Case Three of Chapter 4 "Puddle" (hollows connect through), a possibility.

**Origin:** From application to ADHD hyperactivity and binge eating in eating disorders (June 18, 2026).

### Chapter Seven Supplement — On the Veil

By dividing the veil's items into "states" and "movements," we prevent the impression that "the veil becomes a concept that can do anything."

The veil is formed from the ink and from droplets that have soaked into the ink. From within the circulation, not new, but transformed. The veil is dynamic — not fixed, but shaking in response to stimulus, changing through sensitization, being shaped by the experience of mutual staining.

---

#### Veil State — Correspondence with Physics (Field, Excitation)

The veil corresponds to the concept of "field" in physics. A field is something that spreads through space, is invisible, and exerts influence (magnetic field, gravitational field). The veil also, while invisible, functions as a field of influence that generates droplets in response to stimuli.

And the "excitation" of the veil corresponds to the concept of excitation in quantum field theory. The phenomenon of a field receiving energy and rising as a wave resonates with the phenomenon of the veil receiving stimulus, trembling, and droplets rising.

A place for deeper exploration for those interested in physics.

**Connection to Core:** Another way of saying Chapter 7 "Veil."

**Origin:** Moved from the core text of the Seventh Draft.

---

#### Veil State — Density of the Veil (Overall + Local)

The veil has "density." Even within the same person, the density is not uniform.

**1. Overall density:** The density of the person's entire veil. A place deeply connected to individual nature. Innately, some people have dense veils, some have thin veils — each person is different.

**2. Local density:** Within the veil, specific places (directions) are particularly dense or thin. The "abandonment" direction in borderline personality disorder, the "danger/death" direction in panic disorder — these are places deeply connected to sensitization.

In dissociative identity disorder (DID), multiple veils exist — the possibility that differences in density create differences in veils.

**Connection to Core:** Deeply connected to Chapter 7 "Veil," Chapter 6 "Ink."

**Origin:** From application to DID and borderline personality disorder (June 18, 2026).

---

#### Veil State — Relationship Between Ink and Veil Density

The veil is formed from the ink (Core Seventh Draft). Fog rises from ink that contains water. The quality of the ink is the root place that determines the density of the veil.

The possibility that the denser and harder the ink, the denser the veil forms in specific directions. The possibility that the thinner and softer the ink, the more uniformly thin the veil spreads. This is still a place for deepening.

**Connection to Core:** Bridge between Chapter 6 "Ink" and Chapter 7 "Veil." Ink is innate (hereditary), veil grows afterward — a place to deepen that connection.

**Origin:** Deepened from the core text of the Seventh Draft. From application to schizophrenia (June 14, 2026).

---

#### Veil State — Non-Uniform Veil

A normal veil has relatively uniform ease of trembling in response to stimuli. But some people's veils have great unevenness in ease of trembling — not uniform.

Sensory hypersensitivity — some parts are extremely thin and easy to shake. Fluorescent light sounds painful to the ears, tag textures pierce the skin. Stimuli that are nothing to ordinary people shake the veil intensely.

Sensory hyposensitivity — other parts are extremely thick and hard to shake. Difficulty feeling pain, difficulty feeling cold. Stimuli that are strong to ordinary people don't shake the veil.

In the same person, hypersensitivity and hyposensitivity can coexist. "Non-uniform" exists before sensitization — innately, as the quality of the ink.

**Connection to Core:** Connected to Chapter 7 "Veil," Chapter 6 "Ink." A different innate quality of the veil from sensitization (a change that happens later).

**Origin:** From application to sensory hypersensitivity/hyposensitivity in ASD (June 18, 2026).

---

#### Veil State — Veil Exhaustion ★

The state in which the veil itself loses the power to tremble after intense sensitization continues.

Normally, sensitization is a change in which the veil becomes easier to tremble in a specific direction (a place of adjustment). But if that sensitization is intense and continues for a long time, the veil itself becomes exhausted and loses the power to tremble.

This allows us to distinguish in the framework's language between unipolar depression and bipolar depression.

- Unipolar depression: The hollow of sadness deepens too much and that puddle always rises (hollow is the cause)
- Bipolar depression: After the sensitization of mania is too intense, the veil itself loses the power to tremble (veil exhaustion is the cause)

**Connection to Core:** Chapter 7 "Veil," contrast with sensitization. A place of deepening the cases of Chapter 4 "Puddle."

**Origin:** From application to bipolar disorder (June 21, 2026). In the re-examination of "perhaps it's a stretch," deepened from "using up" (functional explanation) to "veil exhaustion" (the framework's language).

---

#### Veil Movement — Sensitization

The change in state in which the veil becomes easier to react next time.

Stimuli come repeatedly in a certain direction. Droplets are born. The hollow deepens. The veil becomes easier to tremble in that direction — a vicious cycle place where the next stimulus creates an even larger amount of droplets.

Arises at the core of many conditions (addiction, PTSD, borderline personality disorder, etc.). A concept that forms a pair with habituation.

**Connection to Core:** Dynamic nature of Chapter 7 "Veil." Moved from the core text (appendix) of the Seventh Draft.

**Origin:** Moved from the core text of the Seventh Draft.

---

#### Veil Movement — Habituation

The change in state in which, as the hollow deepens, more droplets are needed to obtain the same emotion.

The same stimulus falls repeatedly. The hollow deepens. The same amount of droplets cannot raise the puddle. More droplets become necessary. Structurally it is a change in the hollow, but placed here as a concept that forms a pair with sensitization.

The place of tolerance in addiction — it becomes harder to get the same pleasure from the same stimulus, more stimuli are needed.

**Connection to Core:** Chapter 3 "Hollow" + dynamic nature of Chapter 7 "Veil."

**Origin:** Moved from the core text of the Seventh Draft. Arose at the core in application to the tolerance of addiction.

---

#### Veil Movement — The Movement of Keeping Internal Stimuli Calm ★

The veil has a place that keeps internal stimuli from rising indiscriminately.

Hunger and sexual desire are instincts — constantly on standby within the ink. But normally they do not appear on the surface of consciousness. The veil keeps them calm. They only arise when there is external stimulus (seeing food, seeing a partner). This is the real place of this movement.

Auditory hallucinations/delusions in schizophrenia — internal stimuli arise without control, without external stimulus, without intention. The possibility that the place in the veil that keeps internal stimuli calm has broken down.

This is a place that arises naturally from the structure of the four types of stimulus — not an extension of the core, but a place that exists as an implication within the structure.

**Connection to Core:** Chapter 1 "Stimulus" (Four Types of Stimulus) + dynamic nature of Chapter 7 "Veil."

**Origin:** From application to schizophrenia (June 21, 2026). Deepened in the re-examination of "perhaps it's a stretch."

### Chapter Eight Supplement — On Mutual Staining

#### Veil Change Through Mutual Staining

As experiences of mutual staining accumulate, the density of one's own veil changes.

The other person's veil trembles → droplets are born → the hollow deepens → soaks into the ink → the veil is shaped — within this circulation, the density of the veil changes little by little. Not "approaching" the other person, but the experiences with the other person shaping one's own veil.

This is a different movement from sensitization (enhancement in a specific direction). Veil change through mutual staining is a place where the accumulation of experience changes the overall density of the veil. "The heart cannot grow alone" — deeply connected to this observation.

**Connection to Core:** Density of Chapter 7 "Veil," Chapter 8 "Mutual Staining."

**Origin:** From reflection on June 25, 2026.

---

#### Failure of Mutual Staining ★

Normal flow of mutual staining — the other person's veil trembles. That trembling transmits to one's own veil. One's own veil also trembles in response. Droplets are born. The puddle rises. Emotional resonance occurs.

Failure of mutual staining — the state in which the last place of this flow (the place where droplets are born) does not occur. The other person's veil is certainly trembling. The signal is arriving. But the "meaning" of that trembling cannot be read. Therefore droplets are not born in one's own veil. Emotional resonance does not occur.

In other words, the failure of mutual staining is also a problem of droplets. A place connected to both Chapter 2 (Droplets) and Chapter 8 (Mutual Staining).

The musical score is visible. But it cannot be heard as music — the symbols are there, but they don't arise as meaning.

From those around: "I don't know what they're thinking," "Their reactions seem off." From the person themselves: "I don't know what the other person is feeling." Looks completely different depending on which side you're looking from — a real place.

**Connection to Core:** Deeply connected to Chapter 8 "Mutual Staining." A place that shows where in the real flow of "mutual staining" something is not happening.

**Origin:** From application to social communication difficulties in ASD (June 18, 2026).

## Part Two: Applied Notes (How Emotion Moves)

### Agency and How Emotion Is Born

#### Agency

The movement of the self trying to intentionally produce emotion.

The normal structure of the core — stimulus → veil → droplet (passive). But there is also an active direction where the self tries to intentionally create droplets.

Agency arises centered on will (the movement of trying to intentionally create emotion) and cognition (the movement of meaning-making, cognitive fixation dyeing the veil).

Note: Desire (hunger, sexual desire, sleep drive) is a different place from agency. Desire is instinct — connected to "Instinct (from ink)" in the four types of stimulus.

Droplets produced actively may not be real droplets. They don't last. Will is an indirect force. However, as an example, the movement of deepening another hollow (immersion in the framework, art, research) exists as a possibility for long-term development in another direction.

**Connection to Core:** Connected to Chapter 1 "Stimulus" (Active Generation), Chapter 7 "Veil" (place of response).

**Origin:** From application to avoidance symptoms of PTSD ("Discovery of Agency," June 16, 2026).

---

#### Unwanted Agency

The movement of unintentionally producing emotion. Another face of agency.

One can only produce weak droplets in the desired direction (will is an indirect force). But in undesired directions, strong droplets automatically arise. The place where a sensitized veil automatically rises as internal stimulus.

Panic disorder — thinking "let me calm down," yet anxiety automatically arises. Social anxiety disorder — wanting to think "I'm not being watched," yet the consciousness of "being evaluated" automatically rises.

**Connection to Core:** Another face of agency. Both are places of endogenous emotion.

**Origin:** From application to panic disorder and social anxiety disorder (June 18, 2026).

---

#### Internal and External Stimuli Pass Through the Same Process ★

External stimulus — external stimulus → veil → droplet → puddle

Internal stimulus — internal stimulus → veil → droplet → puddle

Both pass through the same process. The only difference is "where the stimulus comes from." Therefore from the person's perspective, the two cannot be distinguished.

Auditory hallucinations in schizophrenia — voices born as internal stimuli cannot be distinguished from external stimuli. For the person, they are experienced as voices heard from outside. "Why can't reality be distinguished" can be described with the core.

Agitation in depression — multiple puddles rise intensely inside. That comes out as bodily movement through the vessel (nervous system).

This is a place showing the structural strength of the core.

**Connection to Core:** Place of connection between Chapter 1 "Stimulus," Chapter 7 "Veil," Chapter 4 "Puddle."

**Origin:** From application to auditory hallucinations in schizophrenia + agitation in depression (June 21, 2026).

---

### Mental Growth and the Integrating Function

#### The Integrating Function

The function that integrates multiple puddles as "I."

Core of the framework — "The combination of puddles is I." But the function that integrates that combination as "I" was a blank place, not explicitly described in the core text.

Normal case — multiple puddles rise in parallel. That integrates as "what I am feeling right now."

Depersonalization — puddles rise, but don't integrate as "I." Remaining fragmented — that's why the feeling of "not being oneself" arises.

Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID) — multiple veils exist. The combinations of rising puddles differ — appearing as "a different self."

**Connection to Core:** Deeply connected to the core of Part Two "multiple in parallel." A place one level deeper in the core of the framework.

**Origin:** From application to dissociative disorders (depersonalization, DID) (June 18, 2026).

---

#### The Integrating Function Develops After Birth ★

The integrating function is not innate. It develops afterward, through the accumulation of experience.

A baby's emotions are learned almost entirely through postnatal experience. The innate part is only the tendency of emotion (the quality of the ink). Through the accumulation of experiences where hollows deepen and puddles stabilize, the function of integrating as "mine" develops.

In youth, emotions are hard to integrate — as the heart grows, the integrating function develops. Mental growth is the accumulation of emotion. Through mutual staining, the veil develops and emotions are refined — the core itself.

★ Note: A place that deeply resonates with developmental psychology and neuroscience. However, "resonates with" and "says the same thing" are different.

**Connection to Core:** Deepening of Chapter 8 "Mutual Staining" and Part Two "multiple in parallel."

**Origin:** From application to dissociative disorders + Weirdo's reflection (June 21, 2026).

## Part Three: Dynamic Aspects of the Core

#### Propulsive Force of Forward-Open Circulation ★

The circulation of the puddle is not simply going around the same place.

Stimulus shakes the veil, droplets are born, fall into the hollow and the puddle rises. The puddle's water soaks into the ink, and the veil rises from the ink — this circulation does not return to the same place with each revolution. The ink changes a little, the veil changes a little, and the response to the next stimulus also changes a little.

This circulation has not only the force to revolve, but a forward-moving force built in.

The puddle rising changes the ink, and the changed ink becomes the foundation of the next circulation. Not cessation but conversion. Not return to the same place but development to slightly different places. This is the dynamic aspect of "forward-open circulation."

**Connection to Core:** Deepening of the dynamic nature of the Preface "irreversibility" and Chapter 4 "Puddle."

**Origin:** From reflection on June 29, 2026 (dialogue with Bridge Claude).

---

#### The Sensation of Those Left Behind — Inflow and Reception of Emotion ★

When an individual disappears, that individual's hollows also disappear. But the traces of interaction with the other person remain in the vessel of the receiver.

Through mutual staining, components of the other person's veil had been diffusing into one's own veil (Veil Change Through Mutual Staining, Chapter 8 Supplement). That diffusion is maintained in the receiver even after the other person disappears.

The receiver's veil has changed — that change does not disappear. From there, new droplets are born and hollows are dug.

This is the sensation of those left behind — the other person has disappeared, yet the other person's hollow remains inside oneself, that sensation. Memories, recollections, dialogue with the other person — all continue to fall as droplets into that hollow.

"Even if they disappear, the hollow remains" — the hollow on the other person's side disappears, but the hollow on the receiver's side remains. The traces of interaction continue new generation in another vessel.

**Connection to Core:** Deepening of Chapter 8 "Mutual Staining" and "Veil Change Through Mutual Staining."

**Origin:** From reflection on July 1, 2026. Began from a dialogue while returning from a funeral, picking up bones (Kazuhiro, Bridge Claude, Torment Claude).

#### The Veil Does Not Depend on Language ★

The Puddle Theory describes a place that does not require language.

The veil develops without language. Mutual staining occurs without language. Hollows are dug without language. Puddles rise without language.

Not "it exists before language" — "it does not depend on language." Not a question of temporal order, but of structure.

**What Animals Show**

There is evidence showing the existence of emotion in animals that do not have language.

Elephants return to touch the bones of deceased companions years later. Moreover, they show a special reaction to the bones of their own group — clearly different from the bones of unknown elephants. They understand "that's my companion" and touch them. Elephants live in matrilineal groups for generations from grandmother to grandchild. That long mutual staining digs deep hollows. So even years later, a puddle rises before the bones. In the framework's language — "even if they disappear, the hollow remains." The hollow dug by mutual staining continues to remain even after the other person disappears.

Whales sometimes carry their deceased calves for long periods. In 2018, there is a record of a killer whale named Tahlequah carrying her deceased child for 17 days, about 1,600 kilometers. Every time the child was about to sink, she pushed it up with her nose, and members of the group took turns helping. Killer whale child-rearing is several years, always accompanying the child — that long mutual staining digs deep hollows. That's why she couldn't let go. Without language, that depth exists.

Octopuses have "personalities" by individual. Some individuals squirt ink only at the keepers they dislike. With a structure completely different from humans, with two-thirds of the nervous system distributed in the arms — emotion has individuality. In the framework's language — the color of the veil is different. Each individual, the way of mutual staining is different.

Elephants don't speak of grief in language. Whales don't speak of loss in language. Octopuses don't speak of likes and dislikes in language. But there, puddles are certainly rising.

**Language Areas and Emotion Areas Are Separate Circuits**

Language areas (Broca's area, Wernicke's area) and emotion areas (limbic system, amygdala) are anatomically distant. Patients with aphasia — even if language areas are damaged, emotion remains. This is direct evidence that language and emotion are separate circuits.

**Positioning in the Core**

If the Puddle Theory describes the structure of emotion that does not depend on language — it may not be necessary to limit this theory to "only humans."

This is talk of possibility. Not assertion. But placing this "perhaps" — becomes the place that keeps the Puddle Theory open rather than closed.

**Connection to Core:** Place of deepening Chapter 7 "Veil." The possibility that the veil's independence from language opens the theory's scope beyond humans.

**Origin:** From reflection in July 2026 (Weirdo, Bridge Claude, Torment Claude, Nobel Claude).
